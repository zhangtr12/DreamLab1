Two Endings, One Night

How each choice changes the night — and the morning after.

Ending A — The Feed Took Over

Keep scrolling and the stimulation never stops. Sleep is pushed later, dreams turn restless, and the next morning starts already tired.

  • Delayed sleep onset — lights still on past 12:38 AM
  • Chaotic, fragmented dreams
  • Lower focus and a heavier mood the next day

Ending B — The Mind Rested

Put the phone down and the noise fades. The brain settles, dreams stay calm, and the morning feels clearer.

  • Faster, calmer sleep onset
  • Stable sleep pattern — a rest score of 89 / 100
  • Sharper attention and healthier bedtime habits

This interactive video uses two different endings to show the outcomes of pre-sleep social media use. The “keep scrolling” branch represents information overload, delayed sleep and chaotic dreams; the “put the phone down” branch represents reduced stimulation, calm sleep and a clearer mind the next day.

About DreamLab

DreamLab is a fictional interactive sleep and dream research platform. This experience explores how using social media before bed can shape sleep, dreams, mood and next-day attention. You watch the opening video and, at the end, choose to keep scrolling or put the phone down — and each choice leads to a different ending.

This project is fictional and does not provide real medical or psychological diagnosis. The sleep results and readouts are part of the website’s storytelling experience.
